How to Use This Calculator

What are Free Bets?

Free bets are promotional offers from bookmakers where you get a bet without risking your own money. However, there are two main types:

  • Stake Not Returned (SNR): Most common. You only keep the profit if you win. The stake is not returned.
  • Stake Returned (SR): Less common. You keep both the stake and profit if you win.

Quick Guide:

  • Select bet type: Choose SNR (Stake Not Returned) or SR (Stake Returned).
  • Enter free bet amount: The value of your free bet (e.g., $100).
  • Enter odds: The odds for your free bet selection.
  • Enter hedge odds: The odds for the opposite outcome at another bookmaker.
  • Calculate: See the optimal hedge bet and guaranteed profit.

SNR Free Bet Strategy:

For SNR free bets, you want to hedge (bet against yourself) to lock in guaranteed profit:

  1. Place your free bet on one outcome (e.g., Team A to win)
  2. Calculate the optimal hedge bet on the opposite outcome (Team B to win or draw)
  3. Place the hedge bet with real money at another bookmaker
  4. Guaranteed profit regardless of the outcome!

Example Calculation (SNR):

  • Free Bet: $100 SNR
  • Free Bet Odds: 3.00 (Team A)
  • Hedge Odds: 2.50 (Team B)
  • Potential Free Bet Profit: $200 ($300 - $100 stake not returned)
  • Optimal Hedge Bet: $120 (calculated)
  • Guaranteed Profit: $80 regardless of outcome!

Strategy Comparison

Optimal Hedge Strategy

Hedge to lock in guaranteed profit regardless of outcome. Most reliable method.

  • Pros: Guaranteed profit, no risk
  • Cons: Lower profit than unhedged
  • Best for: Risk-averse bettors

No Hedge Strategy

Use free bet without hedging. Higher potential profit but risk of losing everything.

  • Pros: Higher potential profit
  • Cons: Risk losing entire free bet
  • Best for: Risk-tolerant bettors

Partial Hedge Strategy

Hedge only part of the free bet. Balances risk and reward.

  • Pros: Some guaranteed profit + upside
  • Cons: More complex to calculate
  • Best for: Balanced approach

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between SNR and SR free bets?

A: SNR (Stake Not Returned): You only get the profit if you win. SR (Stake Returned): You get both stake and profit if you win. SNR is much more common (90%+ of offers).

Q: Why hedge a free bet?

A: Hedging converts a risky free bet into guaranteed profit. Instead of possibly winning big or losing everything, you lock in a smaller but certain profit.

Q: What's a good conversion rate for SNR free bets?

A: A 70-80% conversion rate is excellent. This means a $100 SNR free bet converts to $70-80 guaranteed profit after hedging. Less than 50% is poor value.

Q: Can I use the same bookmaker for hedging?

A: No! Bookmakers prohibit hedging with them (it's called "matched betting"). Always use a different bookmaker for your hedge bet.

Q: What if the hedge odds change before I bet?

A: Odds can change quickly. Calculate your hedge just before placing it. If odds move unfavorably, you may need to adjust your hedge amount or find different odds.

Q: Are there any risks with free bet hedging?

A: The main risk is odds changing between calculation and betting. Also, bookmakers may restrict accounts if they detect consistent profitable hedging.
