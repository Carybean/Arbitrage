How to Use This Calculator

What is the Kelly Criterion?

The Kelly Criterion is a mathematical formula that determines the optimal bet size to maximize long-term bankroll growth while minimizing the risk of ruin. It balances growth against risk by considering both your edge and the odds offered.

Quick Guide:

  • Enter your bankroll: Total amount you're willing to risk.
  • Enter odds: The bookmaker's odds for your bet (any format).
  • Estimate win probability: Your assessment of the actual win probability.
  • Choose Kelly fraction: Full Kelly (aggressive), Half Kelly (moderate), or Quarter Kelly (conservative).
  • Calculate: See the optimal bet size and risk metrics.

Kelly Fractions:

  • Full Kelly: Maximum growth, higher volatility
  • Half Kelly: 75% of growth with 50% of volatility
  • Quarter Kelly: 50% of growth with 25% of volatility
  • Eighth Kelly: Very conservative, minimal risk

Warning:

Kelly betting requires accurate probability estimates. Overestimating your edge can lead to large losses. Most professionals use fractional Kelly (½ or ¼) to reduce risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the Kelly Criterion?

A: The Kelly Criterion is a mathematical formula that determines the optimal bet size to maximize long-term bankroll growth, given your edge and the odds. It balances growth against risk of ruin.

Q: Why use fractional Kelly?

A: Full Kelly betting has high volatility and assumes perfect probability estimates. Fractional Kelly (½, ¼) reduces volatility and risk while still capturing most of the growth. Most professionals use fractional Kelly.

Q: What if Kelly suggests 0% or negative?

A: If Kelly is 0% or negative, it means the bet has no positive expected value or negative EV. You should not place the bet at all.

Q: How accurate do probability estimates need to be?

A: Very accurate. Overestimating your edge by just a few percentage points can lead to large losses with Kelly betting. Always be conservative in your estimates.

Q: Can I use Kelly for sports betting?

A: Yes, but with caution. Kelly works best when you have a consistent edge. For most recreational bettors, fractional Kelly (¼ or ⅛) is recommended to minimize risk.

Q: What is "risk of ruin"?

A: The probability of losing your entire bankroll. Kelly betting theoretically has 0% risk of ruin, but in practice with estimation errors, fractional Kelly is safer.
